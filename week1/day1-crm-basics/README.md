# Day 1 – CRM Basics
## What is CRM?

CRM stands for Customer Relationship Management.

CRM is a system used by companies to manage customer information, sales, communication, and business workflows in an organized way.

CRM helps businesses:

* Store customer data
* Track leads and sales
* Improve communication
* Automate workflows
* Increase customer satisfaction

---

# Why Companies Use Salesforce

Salesforce is a cloud-based CRM platform used by companies to manage customers and business operations.

Companies use Salesforce to:

* Manage customer information
* Track sales and opportunities
* Improve customer relationships
* Automate business processes
* Generate reports and analytics
* Increase productivity and efficiency

---

# Salesforce Objects

## Account

An Account represents a company or organization.

Example:
College, Hospital, or Business Organization.

---

## Contact

A Contact represents a person associated with an account.

Example:
Student, Patient, or Customer.

---

## Opportunity

An Opportunity represents a possible business deal or sales process.

Example:
Admission process, Treatment package, or Product purchase.

---

# Business Workflow

```text id="h2u8wf"
Lead → Contact → Opportunity → Customer
```

1. A person shows interest in a service or product.
2. The company contacts the person.
3. The person's information is stored as a Contact.
4. A business process or deal starts as an Opportunity.
5. If successful, the person becomes a customer.

---

# Real-World Mapping – College Admission System

Salesforce CRM concepts can be understood using a college admission process.

| Salesforce Object | Real-World Example |
| ----------------- | ------------------ |
| Lead              | Admission enquiry  |
| Contact           | Student            |
| Account           | College            |
| Opportunity       | Admission process  |

## Explanation

* A student asking for admission details is called a Lead.
* The student becomes a Contact after providing complete details.
* The college is considered as the Account.
* The admission discussion and counselling process become an Opportunity.
* After successful admission, the student becomes a customer of the institution.
